About Us

RIT Syllabus Registry is a publicly accessible resource for finding course syllabi for Rochester Institute of Technology courses for undergraduate, graduate, doctorate, and advanced certificate programs. It is intended to be used to give students a better idea of what to expect prior to enrollment in a course, without needing to ask a professor directly for a syllabus.


Some courses have multiple syllabi. These classes are or have been recently taught by multiple professors who use different syllabi. This is most common for lower level classes required by multiple programs (Calculus and Linear Algebra, University Physics, etc.).


Not all syllabi are up to date. This may be because a course is being overhauled, because it is being taught by a new professor, or because it changes semester to semester. If you see a syllabus that is not up to date, please send us a new copy and we will update it as soon as possible.


Not all courses have syllabi listed. If you see a course listed that doesn't have a syllabus, feel free to send us one if you can!


Not all courses listed on the RIT Syllabus Registry are still offered, and most are not offered every semester. We suggest checking with other tools (SIS, CSH Schedule Maker, etc.) to find out if a course you are interested in is being offered.
